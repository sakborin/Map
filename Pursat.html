PURSAT PROVINCE

"Land of Marble and Floating Villages"

At a Glance

Population

397,000

Capital

Pursat City

Best Time to Visit

November - March

Famous For

Marble Carving & Tonle Sap

Pursat, located in central Cambodia along the Tonle Sap Lake, is a province renowned for its marble carving traditions, floating villages, and rich agricultural heritage. Known as the "Land of Marble and Floating Villages," this region offers visitors a unique blend of traditional craftsmanship and aquatic lifestyle. Discover the famous marble carving villages where skilled artisans create intricate sculptures and decorative items, explore the floating villages of Tonle Sap Lake with their unique stilt houses and floating markets, or visit the Cardamom Mountains for eco-tourism adventures. The province's marble quarries have supplied material for temples and monuments throughout Cambodia for centuries. With its renowned craftsmanship, fascinating floating communities, and pristine natural environments, Pursat provides travelers with authentic cultural experiences while showcasing the harmonious relationship between traditional skills and the natural world.
