Kep, Cambodia's smallest province, is a charming seaside retreat famous for its fresh seafood, colonial ruins, and tranquil coastal atmosphere. Known as "The Crab Capital," this picturesque destination offers visitors a perfect blend of culinary delights, historical intrigue, and natural beauty. Savor the world-famous Kep crab at the bustling crab market, explore the haunting ruins of French colonial villas scattered throughout the hills, or take a boat trip to nearby Rabbit Island for pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters. The province's Kep National Park offers scenic hiking trails with panoramic ocean views. With its relaxed coastal charm, exceptional seafood cuisine, and fascinating blend of natural beauty and historical remnants, Kep provides travelers with an intimate and authentic Cambodian seaside experience away from the crowds of larger tourist destinations.
