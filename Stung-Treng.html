Stung Treng, located in northern Cambodia along the Mekong River near the Laotian border, is a province known for its dramatic river rapids, pristine wetlands, and ethnic diversity. Known as "The Northern Frontier," this remote region offers visitors spectacular natural scenery and unique cultural experiences. Navigate the thrilling Mekong rapids and waterfalls, explore the internationally recognized Ramsar wetlands with their incredible biodiversity, or visit traditional ethnic minority villages where Lao, Khmer, and Cham communities coexist. The province's remote location has preserved its natural beauty and traditional ways of life. With its wild river landscapes, protected wetland ecosystems, and multicultural heritage, Stung Treng provides adventurous travelers with authentic wilderness experiences while supporting important conservation efforts that protect the Mekong River's ecological integrity.
