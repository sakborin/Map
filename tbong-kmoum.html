TBOUNG KHMUM PROVINCE

"The Agricultural Heart"

At a Glance

Population

754,000

Capital

Suong

Best Time to Visit

November - March

Famous For

Rubber Plantations & Rice Fields

Tboung Khmum, Cambodia's newest province established in 2013, is a fertile agricultural region in the country's east known for its vast rubber plantations, rice paddies, and traditional farming communities. Separated from Kampong Cham, this province represents the heart of Cambodia's agricultural economy and rural way of life. Experience authentic rural Cambodia by visiting traditional villages where life revolves around rice cultivation and rubber tapping, explore local markets filled with fresh produce, or cycle through endless green fields and plantations. The province's flat terrain and network of waterways create perfect conditions for agriculture and peaceful countryside exploration. With its expansive farmlands, traditional villages, and authentic rural culture, Tboung Khmum provides visitors with genuine insights into Cambodia's agricultural heritage while supporting community-based tourism that benefits local farmers and preserves traditional farming practices in this productive heartland of Cambodian agriculture.
