KRATIE PROVINCE

"Home of the Mekong Dolphins"

At a Glance

Population

319,000

Capital

Kratie City

Best Time to Visit

November - March

Famous For

Irrawaddy Dolphins & Kampi

Kratie, located along the Mekong River in northeastern Cambodia, is a province famous for its rare freshwater dolphins, colonial architecture, and pristine river landscapes. Known as "Home of the Mekong Dolphins," this region offers visitors unique wildlife encounters and authentic riverside experiences. Spot the critically endangered Irrawaddy dolphins at Kampi, one of the few places in the world where these gentle creatures can be observed in their natural habitat, explore the charming French colonial buildings of Kratie town, or take scenic boat trips through the Mekong's dramatic rapids and peaceful stretches. The province's 100 Pillar Pagoda offers panoramic river views. With its rare wildlife, scenic river setting, and well-preserved colonial charm, Kratie provides travelers with extraordinary opportunities to witness endangered species while experiencing the timeless beauty and cultural richness of traditional Mekong River life.
