Preah Vihear, located in northern Cambodia, is a province of ancient temples, dramatic mountain plateaus, and sacred heritage sites. Known as "The Sacred Mountains," this remote region offers visitors access to one of Southeast Asia's most spectacular UNESCO World Heritage temples and pristine wilderness areas. Explore the magnificent Preah Vihear Temple perched dramatically on a 525-meter cliff in the Dângrêk Mountains, discover the ancient wonders of Koh Ker temple complex, or venture through heavily forested landscapes that remain largely untouched by modern development. The province serves as a gateway to Cambodia's most sacred historical sites. With its UNESCO World Heritage temple, ancient Khmer architecture, and breathtaking mountain vistas, Preah Vihear provides history enthusiasts and spiritual seekers with unparalleled access to Cambodia's most revered cultural treasures while preserving the sanctity of these thousand-year-old monuments.
