Crater Lakes & Indigenous Cultures

Ratanakiri, located in northeastern Cambodia, is a province known for its volcanic crater lakes, dense jungles, and diverse indigenous cultures. Known as "The Gem of the Northeast," this remote highland region offers visitors stunning natural beauty and authentic cultural encounters with ethnic minority communities. Swim in the crystal-clear waters of Yeak Laom volcanic crater lake, trek through pristine rainforests home to elephants and rare birds, or visit traditional villages of the Tampuan, Jarai, and Kreung peoples who maintain ancient customs and animist beliefs. The province's volcanic landscape and high elevation create a unique ecosystem unlike anywhere else in Cambodia. With its mystical crater lakes, pristine wilderness, and vibrant indigenous cultures, Ratanakiri provides adventure seekers and cultural enthusiasts with extraordinary highland experiences while supporting ethnic minority communities and conservation efforts that protect Cambodia's most biodiverse forests.
