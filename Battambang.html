"The Rice Bowl of Cambodia"

Battambang, Cambodia's second-largest city, is a charming blend of colonial architecture, rich agricultural heritage, and authentic Khmer culture. Known as the "Rice Bowl of Cambodia," this northwestern province is famous for its fertile plains that produce much of the country's rice supply. Experience the unique bamboo train ride through scenic countryside, explore well-preserved French colonial buildings in the city center, or visit ancient temples like Wat Banan and Phnom Sampeau. The province offers a more relaxed alternative to busier tourist destinations, with traditional villages, artisan workshops, and the famous Battambang Circus showcasing local talent. With its laid-back atmosphere, stunning rural landscapes, and genuine local hospitality, Battambang provides visitors with an authentic glimpse into traditional Cambodian life while serving as a gateway to the country's rich cultural and natural heritage.
